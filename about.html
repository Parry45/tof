About The Okrah Foundation

Who We Are

We are not just a scholarship fund. We are a growing community of people who believe that talent is evenly distributed, but opportunity is not. Founded in 2025 by Kwasi Okrah, we support brilliant African students in their pursuit of world-class education. We believe education is not just personal advancement—it is a lever for collective transformation.

Vision

We imagine a world where no dream is out of reach because of where you were born or the weight of financial constraints. A world where talent, ambition, and drive determine destiny. At The Okrah Foundation, we envision an Africa where access to opportunity is universal, where proof of possibility transforms lives, and where every dream becomes a pathway to limitless potential.

Mission

Our mission is to transform Africa by enabling access to world-class education and experiences for talented individuals who lack the financial means to pursue their dreams. By unlocking doors that seem insurmountable, we connect these individuals with the resources, mentorship, and networks they need to not only realize their ambitions but to drive innovation, leadership, and progress across the continent.

Why It Matters

Every time a brilliant African student gets into a top university and cannot go, we lose more than a degree. We lose an inventor, a founder, a policy shaper, and a future leader.
That is why we do not fund just tuition—we fund future builders.

When I was a boy, I dreamed of flying.

"Not in an aeroplane, but with wings—soaring through the sky, touching the clouds. It was a fantasy, a fairytale—a story my mind conjured, untethered from reality.
‍
But when I dreamed of going to Oxford, standing in classrooms that echoed with the footsteps of history, something strange happened. People treated that dream the same way they treated my childhood fantasy. They laughed. They shook their heads. They said, “That’s not for people like us.”
‍
And for the longest time, I believed them.
‍
We’ve all heard the saying: Dreams don’t work unless you do. It’s inspiring, yes. But it also assumes something fundamental: that everyone believes their dream is possible.
‍
Here lies the challenge for many Africans.
‍
For us, what the world calls “dreams” are often no more than fantasies. When someone says, “I want to go to Harvard,” the response is not one of encouragement. It’s scepticism, pity even. To many, that statement is no different from saying, “I want to be a phoenix” or “I want to be a unicorn.” It’s a fairytale—beautiful, but impossible.
‍
And this is the heart of the problem.
‍
Dreams require belief, a foundation of possibility. Without that, they’re just whispers in the wind.
The shift from fantasy to dream is not automatic. It requires something powerful: evidence. It requires seeing someone who looks like you, talks like you, and comes from where you come from—doing the thing you thought was impossible. It requires a connector.
‍
That connector is what I call proof of possibility. And for many Africans, proof is the missing piece."

Founder’s Story

You got in. But you cannot go.” That is what too many African students hear every year. My name is Kwasi Okrah, and I was almost one of them.
‍
In 2024, I was accepted into the MSc in Law and Finance program at the University of Oxford— a course no Ghanaian man had taken before. I had worked tirelessly, graduated in the top two per cent of my undergraduate class, worked as a finance lawyer and led programs that helped young entrepreneurs rise. But when my funding fell through, it felt like all of that was not enough.
‍
That moment broke something in me—then rebuilt something stronger. I chose not to give up. Instead, I built a foundation that would ensure no brilliant African ever stands at the edge of opportunity and turns back.
This foundation is not about me. It is about all of us who have come close. Who have been told, “Not for people like us.” We are done accepting that. We are rewriting the story.

Oversight Mechanisms

To maintain our commitment to transparency and accountability, we have implemented robust oversight mechanisms:

Annual Financial Audits

We conduct independent audits every year to ensure our financial practices adhere to the highest standards of transparency and accuracy, providing stakeholders with a clear view of how funds are managed.

Quarterly Donor Reports

These reports provide our donors with timely updates on the allocation and use of their contributions, ensuring they are informed about the direct impact of their support.

Accountability Framework

Our foundation has established a comprehensive system to track, measure, and report the outcomes of our initiatives. This framework allows us to evaluate the effectiveness of our programs and continuously improve our operations to maximize positive change.
