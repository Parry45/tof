About The Okrah Foundation

About Us

The Okrah Foundation is a purpose-driven organization dedicated to bridging the gap between ambition and opportunity for young Africans. Our mission is to enable access to world-class education and experiences for talented individuals who lack the financial means to pursue their dreams. Founded by Kwasi Okrah in 2025, the foundation is rooted in a personal journey of overcoming barriers to access higher education at the University of Oxford. This experience fuels our passion for rewriting narratives, proving that "impossible" is just a word.

The foundation is incorporated under the Trustees (Incorporation) Act, 1962 (Act 106) of Ghana.

Vision

We imagine a world where no dream is out of reach because of where you were born or the weight of financial constraints. A world where talent, ambition, and drive determine destiny—not the size of a pocketbook or the whispers of doubt.At The Okrah Foundation, we envision an Africa where access to opportunity is universal, where proof of possibility transforms lives, and where every dream becomes a pathway to limitless potential.

Mission

Our mission is to transform Africa by enabling access to world-class education and experiences for talented individuals who lack the financial means to pursue their dreams. By unlocking doors that seem insurmountable, we connect these individuals with the resources, mentorship, and networks they need to not only realize their ambitions but to drive innovation, leadership, and progress across the continent.

We aim to:

  • Provide Strategic Funding: Support brilliant minds who lack the financial means to seize life-changing opportunities.

  • Inspire Through Proof: Create a ripple effect by showcasing success stories that redefine what’s possible.

  • Build a Legacy of Access: Empower generations of young Africans to pursue their boldest ambitions.

Board of Trustees

Our Board of Trustees is a distinguished team of leaders, each bringing valuable expertise and insight to guide the foundation’s vision and objectives. Together, they ensure that we fulfill our mission with purpose and impact

William Senyo

Project Director
William Senyo

William is the Co-Founder and CEO of Impact Hub Accra. His work is primarily focused on building infrastructure to support entrepreneurs and advising global institutional partners working at the intersection of tech, social impact and the creative economy.

He has spent the last decade leading the vision of Impact Hub Accra. In that role, he has led IHAccra to raise capital from and execute programs for leading global organizations like Facebook, Fondation Botnar, British Council, GiZ, Siemens Stiftung, EU Commission, USAID, IDP Foundation, FMO among others. Will also leads IHA's execution of Ako Adjei Park, a 20,000sqm cluster of 15 live-work-play buildings in a 2sqkm area, designed as the innovation district of the city of Accra to support tech founders, social entrepreneurs and creatives. He also spent 3 years investing in tech-enabled companies through his roles as Venture Partner with Ingressive Capital, a $50M fund.

As a cultural curator, he also hosts global events/festivals such as re:publica Accra and continues to invest in African creatives through FWD, an African creatives fund co-founded with award winning-rapper, M.anifest
Under his leadership, Impact Hub Accra has grown to become Accra’s premier startup hotspot, fostering a diverse community of over 250 members and managing 1700+ square meters of progressively green workspaces.

Working alongside a grassroots movement committed to “DIY Urbanism,” William and his team are focused on activating living and workspaces across Osu Ako Adjei. These transformed spaces bring together local and global ecosystem players to engage with Ghana’s vibrant young entrepreneurs.

William has successfully led teams to raise capital from a range of investors and donors, including: The Rockefeller Foundation, The Tony Elumelu Foundation, The Danish Government, The US Government’s Broadcasting Board of Governors

As a co-convener of Social Enterprise Ghana, William is dedicated to building a robust social enterprise ecosystem in the country. He also serves on the taskforce responsible for drafting Ghana’s first Social Enterprise Policy.

Recognized as a World Economic Forum Global Shaper, William’s efforts exemplify his commitment to creating meaningful impact and driving innovation for the next generation of changemakers.

Oversight Mechanisms

To maintain our commitment to transparency and accountability, we have implemented robust oversight mechanisms:

Annual Financial Audits

We conduct independent audits every year to ensure our financial practices adhere to the highest standards of transparency and accuracy, providing stakeholders with a clear view of how funds are managed.

Quarterly Donor Reports

These reports provide our donors with timely updates on the allocation and use of their contributions, ensuring they are informed about the direct impact of their support.

Accountability Framework

Our foundation has established a comprehensive system to track, measure, and report the outcomes of our initiatives. This framework allows us to evaluate the effectiveness of our programs and continuously improve our operations to maximize positive change.
